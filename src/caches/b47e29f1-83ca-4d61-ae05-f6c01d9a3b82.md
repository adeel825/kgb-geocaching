## Mission Briefing

Greetings, Agent. You are holding in your hands an urgent communique from the Khan Geocaching Bureau — Field Division, Woodland Operations. Our trusted operative, Agent Squirrel, had carefully prepared an entire shipment of prime-grade acorns for delivery to KGB Headquarters. Somewhere between the tree and the drop point, every last acorn vanished. The Bureau is baffled.

## Your Orders, Agent

*— A note, apparently scrawled in haste —*

"Dear Agent,

Oh, goodness — you found it! I have been ever so worried. The acorns were HERE, I promise, neatly bundled and ready to go. Now they are simply... gone. I suspect the wind. Or possibly Gerald (he is a squirrel I do not fully trust).

Your mission, should you choose to accept it, is as follows:

1. Sign your name in the logbook inside. This tells KGB HQ that a reliable agent passed through.
2. Log your find at headquarters (that's the geocaching website, for those new to the Bureau).
3. Tuck this container back exactly where you found it, snug and out of sight. The next agent will need it!

That is all. You have done splendidly."

*— Agent Squirrel, KGB Field Operative (Woodland Division)*

## Field Notes

This is a KGB-003 cache — only the third in our growing network. If you are new to the Bureau, welcome aboard. Every agent starts somewhere, and finding this one means you have the makings of a fine field operative. Keep your eyes open. More missions are coming.

## Sign-Off

Well done, Agent. The Bureau thanks you for your service. Return the shipment point to its natural state and carry on.

*— KGB Seal of Approval —*
