## Mission Briefing

CLASSIFIED — EYES ONLY — KGB FIELD OPERATIVE

Her Majesty Queen Alina of England, Senior Handler and Founding Patron of the Khan Geocaching Bureau, has dispatched an urgent communique to this very location. It seems a vital piece of KGB correspondence was entrusted to the local kangaroo population for safekeeping — the Queen considers them "the most reliable couriers in the Southern Hemisphere, and considerably bouncier than the postal service." Your mission is to recover it. The kangaroos have done their part admirably; now it is your turn.

## Your Orders, Agent

Well done — you have successfully located the drop point and retrieved what the kangaroos were guarding. Queen Alina will be most pleased. Please sign the log sheet within, recording your agent name (or field alias) and today's date. Handle the cache with care and return it precisely as you found it; the kangaroos are watching, and they have a long memory. Once you have logged your visit online, your mission will be marked complete in the KGB field registry.

## Field Notes

Queen Alina established this cache personally during her official tour of the Australian outback, during which she reportedly stopped four times to photograph kangaroos and only once to conduct KGB business. She considers this an appropriate ratio. KGB-007 is the seventh cache in the Bureau's global network — a number the Queen describes as "auspicious, like a very well-organised pouch."
